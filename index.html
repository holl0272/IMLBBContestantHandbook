History

Founded in 1979

The International Mister Leather competition was founded in 1979 by Chuck Renslow and his then partner Dom Orejudos, as a way to increase visibility for the leather community and celebrate the best of our community. As IML contestants, you continue their work and legacy.

1929 – 2017

Charles “Chuck” Renslow

Chuck Renslow was a Chicago-based leather community pioneer, photographer, publisher, and activist. He co-founded International Mr. Leather in 1979 as a celebration of leather culture and community. His vision transformed a local gathering into one of the world’s most respected leather events, drawing contestants and attendees from across the globe.

1933 – 1991

Dom Orejudos

Dom Orejudos — known artistically as Etienne — was a celebrated illustrator and dancer whose work defined the visual language of leather culture. As co-founder of IML and designer of the iconic IML logo in 1979, his artistic vision became inseparable from the identity of the competition and the broader leather community he helped build.

Code of Conduct

International Mr. Leather is committed to running a responsible and transparent event, setting high ethical standards for ourselves and our attendees, supporting each other, and embracing best practices for success and sustainability.

IMLBB Ethics

Key elements of our code of ethics:

Integrity

Being truthful and acting with honesty in all situations.

Confidentiality

Protecting sensitive information about volunteers and attendees.

Professionalism

Maintaining high standards of conduct and competence in one’s responsibilities.

Fairness

Treating everyone equally and without bias.

Respect

Valuing the dignity and worth of others.

Compliance with Laws & Regulations

Adhering to all applicable legal requirements. International Mr. Leather, LLC and IMLBB enforce a zero-tolerance policy regarding physical and sexual offenses, which applies to individuals charged with, indicted for, convicted of, or required to register due to such offenses.

IMLBB Core Values

1

Diversity and Representation

We value individuals who actively work to bring a range of perspectives and voices into conversations, creating a sense of belonging for all. We live at the intersectionality of queer, kinky, leather, and sexual explorer.

2

Insightful Leadership

We demonstrate a depth of understanding about the community, its history, and its evolving needs. We possess the experience to recognize authentic leadership and the potential to inspire meaningful connections. We value actions over words.

3

Emotional Intelligence

We value skilled communicators, capable of distinguishing between dialogue, debate, and conflict, and who know when to engage in each constructively. We appreciate strength of character; however, the ability to take direction is critical.

4

Commitment to Community Building

We believe in fostering relationships and creating spaces where our community and diverse attendees feel welcome. We prioritize the ability to connect, uplift, and mentor others over surface-level attributes.

5

Fairness and Integrity

We are committed to impartiality, transparency, and ethical decision-making, ensuring that everyone is given a fair opportunity to shine. We value individuals who act with honesty, fairness, and strong moral principles, even in difficult situations. We value the courage to do what’s right.

6

Dedication to Growth

We are learners who continually engage with the leather and fetish communities to stay informed about shifting dynamics, challenges, and aspirations. We value self-examination, the ability to challenge assumptions, and to forge new ways forward.

Volunteer Standards

Respectful Behaviour

Treating all individuals, including staff, other volunteers, and beneficiaries, with courtesy and dignity, regardless of background or circumstances.

Confidentiality

Protecting sensitive information about individuals encountered during volunteer work.

Non-Discrimination

Avoiding bias or discriminatory actions based on factors like race, religion, gender, or sexual orientation.

Professionalism

Maintaining a positive attitude, adhering to established policies and procedures, and being punctual and reliable.

Accountability

Taking responsibility for actions and reporting any concerns or ethical breaches to appropriate authorities.

Honesty and Integrity

Acting with transparency and upholding ethical standards in all interactions.

Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Disclosing any potential conflicts of interest that could impact the volunteer role.

Appropriate Communication

Maintaining professional communication channels and avoiding inappropriate language or behaviour.

Why a Volunteer Code of Ethics Matters

Protects Beneficiaries

Ensures volunteers prioritize the well-being and safety of those they serve and work with.

Maintains Organizational Reputation

Upholds the positive image of the organization by ensuring volunteers behave responsibly.

Promotes Positive Volunteer Experience

Provides clear expectations for volunteers, leading to a more fulfilling and ethical volunteering experience.

IML Weekend Code of Conduct

International Mr. Leather (IML) is proud to welcome all leather community members and those who wish to learn more about the leather community, and we acknowledge that we have a responsibility to provide a safe environment. IML welcomes, affirms, and celebrates diversity in all its forms. Every member of the IML Organization is bound by this code of conduct and will respect and honor the dignity of every person who participates in IML Weekend.

We require adherence to this code of conduct as a condition of attending IML. This code of conduct applies to all participants — trustees, staff, volunteers, vendors, contestants, judges, contractors, and attendees. All participants acknowledge that they are attending a sex-positive event that contains some sexually explicit content. All participants of IML must be at least 21 years of age.

🤝

Harassment

IML is dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for all, regardless of age, race, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, personal beliefs, fetish interests or religion. Harassment of any kind is considered a serious breach of this code of conduct.

  • Discrimination or unfair treatment based on any protected characteristic
  • Non-consensual physical contact of any kind
  • Unwelcome sexual attention
  • Deliberate intimidation, threats, stalking, or following

NO means NO. Participants asked to stop harassing behaviour must comply immediately. Contact the help desk on the 1st floor of the hotel.

🚫

Controlled Substances

IML has a zero-tolerance policy regarding illegal substances. Any person believed to be selling or otherwise distributing any controlled substance will be reported to the appropriate authorities.

🚨

Weapons and Replicas

No functioning or realistic appearing firearm may be carried or displayed at any time, regardless of any government permits you may possess. Projectile devices or props which may cause annoyance to others are not allowed.

📷

Photography & Video

Photography and video recording for personal use is generally allowed. Ask permission prior to recording or photographing others — if an individual does not wish to be photographed, their wishes must be respected.

IML has official photographers identified by unique credentials. By entering an IML venue, you may be photographed by them.

📢

Reporting

If you are subject to unacceptable behaviour, notice someone else is being subjected to it, or have concerns this Code of Conduct is not being followed:

  • Contact the help desk on the 1st floor of the hotel
  • Email conduct@imrl.com

All reports will remain confidential. IML does not tolerate retaliation against anyone reporting a violation. Failure to abide by this Code of Conduct may result in removal from the event.
